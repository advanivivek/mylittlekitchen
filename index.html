The “go word” for the decade is sustainability. Chefs are throwing the word around like it’s a new miracle food with some greenwashing consumers in the hope of snaring the extra market dollar.

Maybe the best explanation of Sustainability was from the Bruntdland Commision in 1997, which states it is “development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs”.  If so, then sustainability in our current food system and the food industry needs urgently attention.

How sustainable are restaurants? How sustainable is the next cool ingredient?

3 Pillars of Sustainability

Sustainability must take into consideration the 3 pillars that are like the 3 legs that hold up a table.

ENVIRONMENTAL

How does the way the produce impact the environment, can it be farmed better? Can it use less packaging and less transport?

SOCIAL

The impacts that the produce has on the community & if that product is playing a positive role in rural areas where the jobs are limited.

ECONOMIC

Is the product affordable for the long term of the business. Is it helping in any way to increase or appreciate the bottom line?
